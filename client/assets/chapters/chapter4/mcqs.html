1. The standard form of a quadratic equation is:
(A) ax² + bx + c = 0, a ≠ 0 (B) ax + b = 0 (C) ax³ + bx² + c = 0 (D) None of these
Answer: (A)
2. The degree of a quadratic equation is:
(A) 1 (B) 2 (C) 3 (D) 4
Answer: (B)
3. For a quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0, the discriminant D is:
(A) b² - 4ac (B) b² + 4ac (C) b - 4ac (D) b² - ac
Answer: (A)
4. If b² - 4ac > 0, then the roots of the quadratic equation are:
(A) Real and distinct (B) Real and equal (C) No real roots (D) Imaginary
Answer: (A)
5. If b² - 4ac = 0, then the roots of the quadratic equation are:
(A) Real and distinct (B) Real and equal (C) No real roots (D) None of these
Answer: (B)
6. If b² - 4ac < 0, then the roots of the quadratic equation are:
(A) Real and distinct (B) Real and equal (C) No real roots (D) Real roots
Answer: (C)
7. A quadratic equation can have at most how many real roots?
(A) 1 (B) 2 (C) 3 (D) 4
Answer: (B)
8. Is x = 1 a root of the equation x² - 2x + 1 = 0?
(A) Yes (B) No (C) Cannot say (D) Root is 0
Answer: (A)
9. The quadratic formula to find roots is:
(A) x = \(\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\) (B) x = \(\frac{b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\) (C) x = \(\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 + 4ac}}{2a}\) (D) x = \(\frac{-b \pm b}{2a}\)
Answer: (A)
10. The roots of x² - 9 = 0 are:
(A) 3, -3 (B) 3, 3 (C) -3, -3 (D) 9, -9
Answer: (A)
