1. Solve the following pair of linear equations by the substitution method.
(i) x + y = 14 ; x – y = 4
Solution:
Given equations are:
x + y = 14 ... Let this be equation (1)
x - y = 4 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (2), find x:
x = 4 + y ... Let this be equation (3)
Put this value of x in equation (1):
(4 + y) + y = 14
4 + 2y = 14
2y = 14 - 4
2y = 10
y = \(\frac{10}{2}\)
y = 5
Put y = 5 in equation (3):
x = 4 + 5
x = 9
x = 9, y = 5
(ii) s – t = 3 ; \(\frac{s}{3}\) + \(\frac{t}{2}\) = 6
Solution:
Given equations are:
s - t = 3 ... Let this be equation (1)
\(\frac{s}{3}\) + \(\frac{t}{2}\) = 6 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (1), find s:
s = 3 + t ... Let this be equation (3)
Put this value of s in equation (2):
\(\frac{3 + t}{3}\) + \(\frac{t}{2}\) = 6
LCM of 3 and 2 is 6.
\(\frac{2(3 + t) + 3t}{6}\) = 6
2(3 + t) + 3t = 36
6 + 2t + 3t = 36
6 + 5t = 36
5t = 36 - 6
5t = 30
t = \(\frac{30}{5}\)
t = 6
Put t = 6 in equation (3):
s = 3 + 6
s = 9
s = 9, t = 6
(iii) 3x – y = 3 ; 9x – 3y = 9
Solution:
Given equations are:
3x - y = 3 ... Let this be equation (1)
9x - 3y = 9 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (1), find y:
y = 3x - 3 ... Let this be equation (3)
Put this value of y in equation (2):
9x - 3(3x - 3) = 9
9x - 9x + 9 = 9
9 = 9
This is a true statement.
This means both equations are same.
Infinitely many solutions.
(iv) 0.2x + 0.3y = 1.3 ; 0.4x + 0.5y = 2.3
Solution:
Multiply both equations by 10 to remove decimals.
2x + 3y = 13 ... Let this be equation (1)
4x + 5y = 23 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (1), find x:
2x = 13 - 3y
x = \(\frac{13 - 3y}{2}\) ... Let this be equation (3)
Put this value of x in equation (2):
4[\(\frac{13 - 3y}{2}\)] + 5y = 23
2(13 - 3y) + 5y = 23
26 - 6y + 5y = 23
26 - y = 23
-y = 23 - 26
-y = -3
y = 3
Put y = 3 in equation (3):
x = \(\frac{13 - 3(3)}{2}\)
x = \(\frac{13 - 9}{2}\)
x = \(\frac{4}{2}\)
x = 2
x = 2, y = 3

2. Solve 2x + 3y = 11 and 2x – 4y = – 24 and hence find the value of ‘m’ for which y = mx + 3.
Solution:
Given equations are:
2x + 3y = 11 ... Let this be equation (1)
2x - 4y = -24 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (2), find 2x:
2x = 4y - 24
x = 2y - 12 ... Let this be equation (3)
Put this in equation (1):
2(2y - 12) + 3y = 11
4y - 24 + 3y = 11
7y - 24 = 11
7y = 11 + 24
7y = 35
y = 5
Put y = 5 in equation (3):
x = 2(5) - 12
x = 10 - 12
x = -2
Now we need to find m.
Equation is: y = mx + 3
Put x = -2 and y = 5
5 = m(-2) + 3
5 - 3 = -2m
2 = -2m
m = 2/-2
m = -1
m = -1
3. Form the pair of linear equations for the following problems and find their solution by substitution method.
(i) The difference between two numbers is 26 and one number is three times the other. Find them.
Solution:
Let first number be x.
Let second number be y.
Let x be greater than y.
Difference is 26:
x - y = 26 ... Let this be equation (1)
One number is 3 times other:
x = 3y ... Let this be equation (2)
Put value of x from (2) into (1):
3y - y = 26
2y = 26
y = 13
Put y = 13 in equation (2):
x = 3(13)
x = 39
Numbers are 39 and 13.
(ii) The larger of two supplementary angles exceeds the smaller by 18 degrees. Find them.
Solution:
Let larger angle = x.
Let smaller angle = y.
Angles are supplementary (Sum is 180°):
x + y = 180 ... Let this be equation (1)
Larger exceeds smaller by 18:
x - y = 18 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (2), find x:
x = 18 + y ... Let this be equation (3)
Put in equation (1):
(18 + y) + y = 180
18 + 2y = 180
2y = 180 - 18
2y = 162
y = 81
Put y = 81 in equation (3):
x = 18 + 81
x = 99
Angles are 99° and 81°.
(iii) The coach of a cricket team buys 7 bats and 6 balls for ₹ 3800. Later, she buys 3 bats and 5 balls for ₹ 1750. Find the cost of each bat and each ball.
Solution:
Let cost of 1 bat = ₹x
Let cost of 1 ball = ₹y
Case 1: 7 bats + 6 balls = 3800
7x + 6y = 3800 ... Let this be equation (1)
Case 2: 3 bats + 5 balls = 1750
3x + 5y = 1750 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (2), find x:
3x = 1750 - 5y
x = \(\frac{1750 - 5y}{3}\) ... Let this be equation (3)
Put in equation (1):
7[\(\frac{1750 - 5y}{3}\)] + 6y = 3800
[\(\frac{12250 - 35y}{3}\)] + 6y = 3800
Multiply by 3:
12250 - 35y + 18y = 11400
12250 - 17y = 11400
-17y = 11400 - 12250
-17y = -850
y = \(\frac{850}{17}\)
y = 50
Put y = 50 in equation (3):
x = \(\frac{1750 - 5(50)}{3}\)
x = \(\frac{1750 - 250}{3}\)
x = \(\frac{1500}{3}\)
x = 500
Cost of bat = ₹500, Cost of ball = ₹50.
(iv) The taxi charges in a city consist of a fixed charge together with the charge for the distance covered. For a distance of 10 km, the charge paid is ₹ 105 and for a journey of 15 km, the charge paid is ₹ 155. What are the fixed charges and the charge per km? How much does a person have to pay for travelling a distance of 25 km?
Solution:
Let fixed charge = ₹x
Let charge per km = ₹y
For 10 km journey:
Fixed + 10 × (per km) = 105
x + 10y = 105 ... Let this be equation (1)
For 15 km journey:
x + 15y = 155 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (1), find x:
x = 105 - 10y ... Let this be equation (3)
Put in equation (2):
(105 - 10y) + 15y = 155
105 + 5y = 155
5y = 155 - 105
5y = 50
y = 10
Put y = 10 in equation (3):
x = 105 - 10(10)
x = 105 - 100
x = 5
Cost for 25 km = x + 25y
= 5 + 25(10)
= 5 + 250 = 255
Fixed charge = ₹5, Charge per km = ₹10. Total cost = ₹255.
(v) A fraction becomes \(\frac{9}{11}\) if 2 is added to both the numerator and the denominator. If 3 is added to both the numerator and the denominator it becomes \(\frac{5}{6}\). Find the fraction.
Solution:
Let numerator = x.
Let denominator = y.
Fraction = \(\frac{x}{y}\).
Case 1: Add 2 to both.
\(\frac{x + 2}{y + 2}\) = \(\frac{9}{11}\)
11(x + 2) = 9(y + 2)
11x + 22 = 9y + 18
11x - 9y = -4 ... Let this be equation (1)
Case 2: Add 3 to both.
\(\frac{x + 3}{y + 3}\) = \(\frac{5}{6}\)
6(x + 3) = 5(y + 3)
6x + 18 = 5y + 15
6x - 5y = -3 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (1), find x:
11x = 9y - 4
x = \(\frac{9y - 4}{11}\) ... Let this be equation (3)
Put in equation (2):
6[\(\frac{9y - 4}{11}\)] - 5y = -3
[\(\frac{54y - 24}{11}\)] - 5y = -3
54y - 24 - 55y = -33 (Multiplying by 11)
-y - 24 = -33
-y = -33 + 24
-y = -9 => y = 9
Put y = 9 in equation (3):
x = \(\frac{9(9) - 4}{11}\)
x = \(\frac{81 - 4}{11}\)
x = \(\frac{77}{11}\)
x = 7
Fraction is \(\frac{7}{9}\).
(vi) Five years hence, the age of Jacob will be three times that of his son. Five years ago, Jacob’s age was seven times that of his son. What are their present ages?
Solution:
Let Jacob's age = x.
Let Son's age = y.
5 years hence (Future):
x + 5 = 3(y + 5)
x + 5 = 3y + 15
x - 3y = 10 ... Let this be equation (1)
5 years ago (Past):
x - 5 = 7(y - 5)
x - 5 = 7y - 35
x - 7y = -30 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (1), find x:
x = 10 + 3y ... Let this be equation (3)
Put in equation (2):
(10 + 3y) - 7y = -30
10 - 4y = -30
-4y = -40
y = 10
Put y = 10 in equation (3):
x = 10 + 3(10)
x = 40
Jacob is 40 years old, Son is 10 years old.
