(i) 10 students of Class X took part in a Mathematics quiz. If the number of girls is 4 more than the number of boys, find the number of boys and girls who took part in the quiz.
Given:
Total students = 10
Number of girls is 4 more than the number of boys.
To Find:
Number of boys and girls.
Solution:

Let number of boys = y

1. Solve the following pair of linear equations by the substitution method.
(i) x + y = 14 ; x y = 4
Solution:
Given equations are:
x + y = 14 ... Let this be equation (1)
x - y = 4 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (2), find x:
x = 4 + y ... Let this be equation (3)
Put this value of x in equation (1):
(4 + y) + y = 14
4 + 2y = 14
2y = 14 - 4
2y = 10
y = \(\frac{10}{2}\)
y = 5
Put y = 5 in equation (3):
x = 4 + 5
x = 9
x = 9, y = 5
(ii) s t = 3 ; \(\frac{s}{3}\) + \(\frac{t}{2}\) = 6
Solution:
Given equations are:
s - t = 3 ... Let this be equation (1)
\(\frac{s}{3}\) + \(\frac{t}{2}\) = 6 ... Let this be equation (2)
From equation (1), find s:
s = 3 + t ... Let this be equation (3)
Put this value of s in equation (2):
\(\frac{3 + t}{3}\) + \(\frac{t}{2}\) = 6
LCM of 3 and 2 is 6.
\(\frac{2(3 + t) + 3t}{6}\) = 6
2(3 + t) + 3t = 36
6 + 2t + 3t = 36
6 + 5t = 36
5t = 36 - 6
5t = 30
t = \(\frac{30}{5}\)
t = 6
Put t = 6 in equation (3):
s = 3 + 6
s = 9
s = 9, t = 6

This is a true statement.
This means both equations are same.
Infinitely many solutions.

(i) x + y = 5 and 2x 3y = 4
Solution:
x + y = 5 ...(1)
2x - 3y = 4 ...(2)

3x + 3y = 15 ...(3)

Substitute x in (1):
\(\frac{19}{5}\) + y = 5
y = 5 - \(\frac{19}{5}\)
y = \(\frac{25 - 19}{5}\)
y = \(\frac{6}{5}\)
x = \(\frac{19}{5}\), y = \(\frac{6}{5}\)
(ii) 3x + 4y = 10 and 2x 2y = 2
Solution:
3x + 4y = 10 ...(1)
2x - 2y = 2 ...(2)
Multiply (2) by 2:
4x - 4y = 4 ...(3)
Add (1) and (3):
3x + 4y + 4x - 4y = 10 + 4
7x = 14
x = 2
Substitute x = 2 in (2):
2(2) - 2y = 2
4 - 2y = 2
-2y = -2
y = 1
x = 2, y = 1
(iii) 3x 5y 4 = 0 and 9x = 2y + 7

Denominator + 1 Fraction = \(\frac{1}{2}\)

Example 1. Check graphically whether x + 3y = 6 and 2x - 3y = 12 is consistent. If so, solve them graphically.
Solution:
Given equations:
x + 3y = 6 ...(1)
2x - 3y = 12 ...(2)
Observation for Graph:
For equation (1):
x = 6 - 3y
x 6 0
y 0 2
For equation (2):
2x = 12 + 3y
x = \(\frac{12 + 3y}{2}\)
x 6 0
y 0 -4
Plotting the points, both lines intersect at the point (6, 0).
Since there is a unique point of intersection, the pair of equations is consistent.
Solution: x = 6, y = 0
Example 2. Graphically, find whether the following pair of equations has no solution, unique solution or infinitely many solutions:
5x - 8y + 1 = 0
3x - \(\frac{24}{5}\)y + \(\frac{3}{5}\) = 0
Solution:
Given equations:
5x - 8y + 1 = 0 ...(1)
3x - \(\frac{24}{5}\)y + \(\frac{3}{5}\) = 0 ...(2)
Simplify equation (2) by multiplying by 5:
15x - 24y + 3 = 0
Divide by 3:
5x - 8y + 1 = 0 ...(3)
Equation (1) and (3) are the same.
Comparing ratios:
\(\frac{a1}{a2}\) = \(\frac{5}{5}\) = 1
\(\frac{b1}{b2}\) = \(\frac{-8}{-8}\) = 1
\(\frac{c1}{c2}\) = \(\frac{1}{1}\) = 1
Since ratios are equal, lines are coincident.
Infinitely many solutions.
Example 3. Champa went to a 'Sale' to purchase some pants and skirts. When her friends asked her how many of each she had bought, she answered, "The number of skirts is two less than twice the number of pants purchased. Also, the number of skirts is four less than four times the number of pants purchased". Help her friends to find how many pants and skirts Champa bought.
Solution:
Let number of pants = x.
Let number of skirts = y.
Condition 1: Skirts is 2 less than twice pants.
y = 2x - 2 ...(1)
Condition 2: Skirts is 4 less than 4 times pants.
y = 4x - 4 ...(2)
Substitute value of y from (1) into (2):
2x - 2 = 4x - 4
4 - 2 = 4x - 2x
2 = 2x
x = 1
Put x = 1 in (1):
y = 2(1) - 2 = 0
Champa bought 1 pant and 0 skirts.
Example 4. Solve the following pair of equations by substitution method:
7x - 15y = 2
x + 2y = 3
Solution:
7x - 15y = 2 ...(1)
x + 2y = 3 ...(2)
From (2):
x = 3 - 2y ...(3)
Substitute x in (1):
7(3 - 2y) - 15y = 2
21 - 14y - 15y = 2
21 - 29y = 2
-29y = -19
y = \(\frac{19}{29}\)
Substitute y in (3):
x = 3 - 2(\(\frac{19}{29}\))
x = 3 - \(\frac{38}{29}\)
x = \(\frac{87 - 38}{29}\)
x = \(\frac{49}{29}\)
x = \(\frac{49}{29}\), y = \(\frac{19}{29}\)
Example 5. Aftab tells his daughter, "Seven years ago, I was seven times as old as you were then. Also, three years from now, I shall be three times as old as you will be." (Isn't this interesting?) Solve by the method of substitution.
Solution:
Let Aftab's present age = x.
Let Daughter's present age = y.
Seven years ago:
x - 7 = 7(y - 7)
x - 7 = 7y - 49
x - 7y = -42 ...(1)
Three years from now:
x + 3 = 3(y + 3)
x + 3 = 3y + 9
x - 3y = 6 ...(2)
From (2): x = 6 + 3y ...(3)
Substitute in (1):
(6 + 3y) - 7y = -42
6 - 4y = -42
-4y = -48
y = 12
Put y = 12 in (3):
x = 6 + 3(12) = 42
Aftab is 42 years old, Daughter is 12 years old.
Example 6. Romila went to a stationery shop and purchased 2 pencils & 3 erasers for Rs 9. Her friend Sonali saw the new variety of pencils and erasers with Romila, and she also bought 4 pencils and 6 erasers of the same kind for Rs 18. Find the cost of each pencil and each eraser.
Solution:
Let cost of 1 pencil = Rs x.
Let cost of 1 eraser = Rs y.
Romila's purchase:
2x + 3y = 9 ...(1)
Sonali's purchase:
4x + 6y = 18 ...(2)
From (1): 3y = 9 - 2x => y = \(\frac{9 - 2x}{3}\)
Substitute in (2):
4x + 6(\(\frac{9 - 2x}{3}\)) = 18
4x + 2(9 - 2x) = 18
4x + 18 - 4x = 18
18 = 18
This is always true.
Both equations represent the same line.
Infinitely many solutions (Cost is not unique).
Example 7. Two rails are represented by the equations x + 2y 4 = 0 and 2x + 4y 12 = 0. Will the rails cross each other?
Solution:
Equation 1: x + 2y - 4 = 0
Equation 2: 2x + 4y - 12 = 0
Divide Equation 2 by 2:
x + 2y - 6 = 0
Compare coefficients:
\(\frac{a1}{a2}\) = \(\frac{1}{2}\)
\(\frac{b1}{b2}\) = \(\frac{2}{4}\) = \(\frac{1}{2}\)
\(\frac{c1}{c2}\) = \(\frac{-4}{-12}\) = \(\frac{1}{3}\)
\(\frac{a1}{a2}\) = \(\frac{b1}{b2}\) \(\frac{c1}{c2}\)
The lines are parallel.
No, the rails will not cross each other.
Example 8. The ratio of incomes of two persons is 9 : 7 and the ratio of their expenditures is 4 : 3. If each of them manages to save Rs.2000 per month, find their monthly incomes.
Solution:
Let incomes be 9x and 7x.
Let expenditures be 4y and 3y.
Savings = Income - Expenditure = 2000
Person 1: 9x - 4y = 2000 ...(1)
Person 2: 7x - 3y = 2000 ...(2)
Multiply (1) by 3 and (2) by 4 to eliminate y:
(1) x 3 => 27x - 12y = 6000 ...(3)
(2) x 4 => 28x - 12y = 8000 ...(4)
Subtract (3) from (4):
(28x - 27x) + (-12y + 12y) = 8000 - 6000
x = 2000
Incomes are 9x and 7x.
9(2000) = 18000
7(2000) = 14000
Monthly incomes are Rs. 18,000 and Rs. 14,000.

Example 10. The sum of a two-digit number and the number obtained by reversing the digits is 66. If the digits of the number differ by 2, find the number. How many such numbers are there?
Solution:
Let ten's digit = x, unit's digit = y.
Number = 10x + y
Reversed Number = 10y + x
Sum = 66
(10x + y) + (10y + x) = 66
11x + 11y = 66
Divide by 11: x + y = 6 ...(1)
Digits differ by 2. So |x - y| = 2.
Case 1: x - y = 2 ...(2)
Add (1) and (2):
2x = 8 => x = 4
Put x=4 in (1): 4 + y = 6 => y = 2
Number is 42.
Case 2: y - x = 2 ...(3)
Add (1) and (3) (Note: (3) is -x + y = 2):
2y = 8 => y = 4
Put y=4 in (1): x + 4 = 6 => x = 2
Number is 24.
Numbers are 42 and 24. There are two such numbers.
