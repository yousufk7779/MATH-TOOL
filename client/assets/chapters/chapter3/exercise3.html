1. Solve the following pair of linear equations by the elimination method and the substitution method.
(i) x + y = 5 and 2x – 3y = 4
Solution:
x + y = 5 ...(1)
2x - 3y = 4 ...(2)
Multiply (1) by 3:
3x + 3y = 15 ...(3)
Add (2) and (3):
2x - 3y + 3x + 3y = 4 + 15
5x = 19
x = \(\frac{19}{5}\)
Substitute x in (1):
\(\frac{19}{5}\) + y = 5
y = 5 - \(\frac{19}{5}\)
y = \(\frac{25 - 19}{5}\)
y = \(\frac{6}{5}\)
x = \(\frac{19}{5}\), y = \(\frac{6}{5}\)
(ii) 3x + 4y = 10 and 2x – 2y = 2
Solution:
3x + 4y = 10 ...(1)
2x - 2y = 2 ...(2)
Multiply (2) by 2:
4x - 4y = 4 ...(3)
Add (1) and (3):
3x + 4y + 4x - 4y = 10 + 4
7x = 14
x = 2
Substitute x = 2 in (2):
2(2) - 2y = 2
4 - 2y = 2
-2y = -2
y = 1
x = 2, y = 1
(iii) 3x – 5y – 4 = 0 and 9x = 2y + 7
Solution:
3x - 5y = 4 ...(1)
9x - 2y = 7 ...(2)
Multiply (1) by 3:
9x - 15y = 12 ...(3)
Subtract (3) from (2):
(9x - 2y) - (9x - 15y) = 7 - 12
13y = -5
y = \(\frac{-5}{13}\)
Substitute y in (1):
3x - 5(\(\frac{-5}{13}\)) = 4
3x + \(\frac{25}{13}\) = 4
3x = 4 - \(\frac{25}{13}\)
3x = \(\frac{52 - 25}{13}\)
x = \(\frac{9}{13}\)
x = \(\frac{9}{13}\), y = \(\frac{-5}{13}\)
(iv) \(\frac{x}{2}\) + \(\frac{2y}{3}\) = -1 and x - \(\frac{y}{3}\) = 3
Solution:
Multiply eq 1 by 6: 3x + 4y = -6 ...(1)
Multiply eq 2 by 3: 3x - y = 9 ...(2)
Subtract (2) from (1):
(3x + 4y) - (3x - y) = -6 - 9
5y = -15
y = -3
Substitute y = -3 in (2):
3x - (-3) = 9
3x + 3 = 9
3x = 6
x = 2
x = 2, y = -3
2. Form the pair of linear equations in the following problems, and find their solutions (if they exist) by the elimination method:
(i) If we add 1 to the numerator and subtract 1 from the denominator, a fraction reduces to 1. It becomes \(\frac{1}{2}\) if we only add 1 to the denominator. What is the fraction?
Given:
Numerator + 1, Denominator - 1 ⇒ Fraction = 1
Denominator + 1 ⇒ Fraction = \(\frac{1}{2}\)
Solution:
Let fraction be \(\frac{x}{y}\)
\(\frac{x + 1}{y - 1}\) = 1
x + 1 = y - 1
x - y = -2 ...(1)
\(\frac{x}{y + 1}\) = \(\frac{1}{2}\)
2x = y + 1
2x - y = 1 ...(2)
Subtract (1) from (2):
(2x - y) - (x - y) = 1 - (-2)
x = 3
Substitute x = 3 in (1):
3 - y = -2
y = 5
Fraction is \(\frac{3}{5}\)
(ii) Five years ago, Nuri was thrice as old as Sonu. Ten years later, Nuri will be twice as old as Sonu. How old are Nuri and Sonu?
Given:
5 years ago: Nuri = 3 × Sonu
10 years later: Nuri = 2 × Sonu
Solution:
Let Nuri's age = x, Sonu's age = y
x - 5 = 3(y - 5)
x - 3y = -10 ...(1)
x + 10 = 2(y + 10)
x - 2y = 10 ...(2)
Subtract (1) from (2):
(x - 2y) - (x - 3y) = 10 - (-10)
y = 20
Substitute y = 20 in (2):
x - 40 = 10 ⇒ x = 50
Nuri is 50 years old, Sonu is 20 years old.
(iii) The sum of the digits of a two-digit number is 9. Also, nine times this number is twice the number obtained by reversing the order of the digits. Find the number.
Solution:
Let number = 10x + y (x is tens digit, y is units digit).
x + y = 9 ...(1)
9(10x + y) = 2(10y + x)
90x + 9y = 20y + 2x
88x - 11y = 0
8x - y = 0 ...(2)
Add (1) and (2):
9x = 9
x = 1
Substitute x = 1 in (1)
y = 8
Number is 18.
(iv) Meena went to a bank to withdraw ₹2000. She asked the cashier to give her ₹50 and ₹100 notes only. Meena got 25 notes in all. Find how many notes of ₹50 and ₹100 she received.
Solution:
Let notes of ₹50 = x, ₹100 = y.
Total notes: x + y = 25 ...(1)
Total value: 50x + 100y = 2000
x + 2y = 40 ...(2)
Subtract (1) from (2):
y = 15
Substitute y = 15 in (1):
x = 10
10 notes of ₹50, 15 notes of ₹100.
(v) A lending library has a fixed charge for the first three days and an additional charge for each day thereafter. Saritha paid Rs.27 for a book kept for seven days, while Susy paid Rs.21 for the book she kept for five days. Find the fixed charge and the charge for each extra day.
Solution:
Let fixed charge (3 days) = x, extra day charge = y.
Saritha (7 days): x + 4y = 27 ...(1)
Susy (5 days): x + 2y = 21 ...(2)
Subtract (2) from (1):
2y = 6
y = 3
Substitute y = 3 in (2):
x + 6 = 21
x = 15
Fixed charge = ₹ 15, Extra day charge = ₹ 3.
