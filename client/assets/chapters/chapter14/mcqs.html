1. The probability of an impossible event is:
(a) 1
(b) \(\frac{1}{2}\)
(c) 0
(d) Not defined
Answer: (c) 0
An impossible event never happens.
2. Which of the following cannot be the probability of an event?
(a) 0.1
(b) 3%
(c) \(\frac{17}{16}\)
(d) 0.001
Answer: (c) \(\frac{17}{16}\)
\(\frac{17}{16}\) = 1.0625, which is > 1. Probability must be <= 1.
3. Two coins are tossed simultaneously. What is the probability of getting at most one head?
(a) \(\frac{1}{4}\)
(b) \(\frac{1}{2}\)
(c) \(\frac{3}{4}\)
(d) 1
Answer: (c) \(\frac{3}{4}\)
Outcomes: HH, HT, TH, TT. At most one head: HT, TH, TT. Count 3.
4. A card is drawn from a pack of 52 cards. The probability that it is a face card is:
(a) \(\frac{4}{13}\)
(b) \(\frac{3}{13}\)
(c) \(\frac{2}{13}\)
(d) \(\frac{1}{13}\)
Answer: (b) \(\frac{3}{13}\)
12 Face \(\frac{cards}{52}\) = \(\frac{3}{13}\).
5. If P(E) = 0.07, then P(not E) is:
(a) 0.93
(b) 0.03
(c) 0.97
(d) 0
Answer: (a) 0.93
1 - 0.07 = 0.93.
6. A die is thrown once. The probability of getting a number less than 3 is:
(a) \(\frac{1}{3}\)
(b) \(\frac{1}{2}\)
(c) \(\frac{1}{6}\)
(d) \(\frac{2}{3}\)
Answer: (a) \(\frac{1}{3}\)
Less than 3: {1, 2}. Count = 2. P = \(\frac{2}{6}\) = \(\frac{1}{3}\).
7. The probability of a certain event is:
(a) 0
(b) 1
(c) > 1
(d) < 1
Answer: (b) 1
Definition of Sure Event.
8. Which of the following can be probability of an event?
(a) -0.4
(b) 1.004
(c) \(\frac{18}{23}\)
(d) \(\frac{8}{7}\)
Answer: (c) \(\frac{18}{23}\)
Between 0 and 1.
9. Three coins are tossed. Probability of getting exactly two tails is:
(a) \(\frac{1}{8}\)
(b) \(\frac{3}{8}\)
(c) \(\frac{1}{2}\)
(d) \(\frac{1}{4}\)
Answer: (b) \(\frac{3}{8}\)
Outcomes: TTH, THT, HTT. Count 3.
10. A letter is chosen at random from the word 'MATHEMATICS'. The probability that it is a vowel is:
(a) \(\frac{4}{11}\)
(b) \(\frac{3}{11}\)
(c) \(\frac{5}{11}\)
(d) \(\frac{2}{11}\)
Answer: (a) \(\frac{4}{11}\)
M, A, T, H, E, M, A, T, I, C, S (11 total). Vowels: A, E, A, I (4).
