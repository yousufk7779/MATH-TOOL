1. A tangent PQ at a point P of a circle of radius 5 cm meets a line through the centre O at a point Q so that OQ = 12 cm. Length PQ is :
(a) 12 cm
(b) 13 cm
(c) 8.5 cm
(d) √119 cm
Answer: (d) √119 cm
2. From a point Q, the length of the tangent to a circle is 24 cm and the distance of Q from the centre is 25 cm. The radius of the circle is :
(a) 7 cm
(b) 12 cm
(c) 15 cm
(d) 24.5 cm
Answer: (a) 7 cm
3. If tangents PA and PB from a point P to a circle with centre O are inclined to each other at angle of 80°, then ∠ POA is equal to :
(a) 50°
(b) 60°
(c) 70°
(d) 80°
Answer: (a) 50°
4. If TP and TQ are the two tangents to a circle with centre O so that ∠ POQ = 110°, then ∠ PTQ is equal to :
(a) 60°
(b) 70°
(c) 80°
(d) 90°
Answer: (b) 70°
5. The length of a tangent from a point A at distance 5 cm from the centre of the circle is 4 cm. The radius of the circle is:
(a) 3 cm
(b) 5 cm
(c) 7 cm
(d) 3.5 cm
Answer: (a) 3 cm
6. Two concentric circles are of radii 5 cm and 3 cm. The length of the chord of the larger circle which touches the smaller circle is:
(a) 8 cm
(b) 10 cm
(c) 12 cm
(d) 18 cm
Answer: (a) 8 cm
7. A parallelogram circumscribing a circle is a:
(a) Square
(b) Rectangle
(c) Rhombus
(d) Trapezium
Answer: (c) Rhombus
8. The angle between two radii of a circle is 130°, the angle between the tangents at the ends of the radii is:
(a) 90°
(b) 50°
(c) 70°
(d) 40°
Answer: (b) 50°
9. If PQR is a tangent to a circle at Q whose centre is O, AB is a chord parallel to PR and ∠ BQR = 70°, then ∠ AQB is equal to:
(a) 20°
(b) 40°
(c) 35°
(d) 45°
Answer: (b) 40°
10. At one end A of a diameter AB of a circle of radius 5 cm, tangent XAY is drawn to the circle. The length of the chord CD parallel to XY and at a distance 8 cm from A is:
(a) 4 cm
(b) 5 cm
(c) 6 cm
(d) 8 cm
Answer: (d) 8 cm
