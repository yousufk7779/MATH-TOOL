Chapter 2: Polynomials
1. Introduction
A polynomial is an algebraic expression consisting of variables and coefficients, that involves only the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and non-negative integer exponents of variables.
In this chapter, we will study the geometrical meaning of zeroes of a polynomial and the relationship between zeroes and coefficients of a polynomial.
2. Types of Polynomials
A. Based on Number of Terms:
  • Monomial: A polynomial having exactly one term. (e.g., 2x, 5, 3x²)
  • Binomial: A polynomial having exactly two non-zero terms. (e.g., x + 2, x² - 5)
  • Trinomial: A polynomial having exactly three non-zero terms. (e.g., x² + 2x + 1)
B. Based on Degree:
The degree of a polynomial is the highest power of the variable in it.
  • Constant Polynomial: A polynomial of degree 0. (e.g., 5, -2)
  • Linear Polynomial: A polynomial of degree 1. Standard form: ax + b. (e.g., 2x - 3)
  • Quadratic Polynomial: A polynomial of degree 2. Standard form: ax² + bx + c. (e.g., 2x² + 3x - 5)
  • Cubic Polynomial: A polynomial of degree 3. Standard form: ax³ + bx² + cx + d. (e.g., x³ - 2x + 4)
3. Important Terms
Zero of a Polynomial: A real number k is said to be a zero of a polynomial p(x), if p(k) = 0.
Value of a Polynomial: If p(x) is a polynomial in x, and if k is any real number, then the value obtained by replacing x by k in p(x), is called the value of p(x) at x = k, and is denoted by p(k).
4. Geometrical Meaning of Zeroes
The zero of a polynomial p(x) is the x-coordinate of the point where the graph of y = p(x) intersects the X-axis.
  • For a linear polynomial ax + b, the graph is a straight line intersecting the X-axis at exactly one point: (-b/a, 0).
  • For a quadratic polynomial ax² + bx + c, the graph is a parabola opening upwards (if a > 0) or downwards (if a < 0). It can intersect the X-axis at at most two points.
  • In general, a polynomial p(x) of degree n has at most n zeroes.
5. Relationship between Zeroes and Coefficients
For a Quadratic Polynomial p(x) = ax² + bx + c, let α and β be the zeroes.
Sum of zeroes (α + β) = - (Coefficient of x) / (Coefficient of x²) = -b / a
Product of zeroes (α × β) = (Constant term) / (Coefficient of x²) = c / a
If sum (S) and product (P) of zeroes are given, the quadratic polynomial is given by:
k[x² - (α + β)x + αβ]
OR
k[x² - Sx + P]
where k is any non-zero real number.
6. Cubic Polynomials (Optional)
For a Cubic Polynomial ax³ + bx² + cx + d with zeroes α, β, and γ:
  • Sum of zeroes (α + β + γ) = -b/a
  • Sum of product of zeroes taken two at a time (αβ + βγ + γα) = c/a
  • Product of zeroes (αβγ) = -d/a
