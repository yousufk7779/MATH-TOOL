Chapter 13: Important MCQs
1. The relationship between Mean, Median and Mode for a moderately skewed distribution is:
(a) Mode = 2 Median - 3 Mean
(b) Mode = Median - 2 Mean
(c) Mode = 3 Median - 2 Mean
(d) Mode = 2 Mean - 3 Median
Answer: (c) Mode = 3 Median - 2 Mean
Empirical formula.
2. The mode of the following data: 2, 6, 4, 5, 0, 2, 1, 3, 2, 3 is:
(a) 2
(b) 3
(c) 4
(d) 5
Answer: (a) 2
2 appears most frequently (3 times).
3. If the mean of the following distribution is 6, find the value of p: x: 2 4 6 10 p+5; f: 3 2 3 1 2
(a) 6
(b) 7
(c) 5
(d) 4
Answer: (b) 7
Sum f = 11. Sum fx = 6+8+18+10+2p+10 = 52+2p. Mean = (52+2p)/11 = 6. 52+2p=66 => 2p=14 => p=7.
4. The algebraic sum of deviations of a frequency distribution from its mean is:
(a) Always positive
(b) Always negative
(c) Zero
(d) Non-zero
Answer: (c) Zero
Property of Mean: Σ(x - x̄) = 0.
5. In the formula x̄ = a + (Σfᵢuᵢ/Σfᵢ) × h, uᵢ is:
(a) xᵢ - a
(b) (xᵢ - a) / h
(c) (a - xᵢ) / h
(d) h(xᵢ - a)
Answer: (b) (xᵢ - a) / h
Step deviation definition.
6. While computing mean of grouped data, we assume that the frequencies are:
(a) centred at the upper limits of the classes
(b) centred at the lower limits of the classes
(c) centred at the classmarks of the classes
(d) evenly distributed
Answer: (c) centred at the classmarks of the classes
We take midpoint xi.
7. Construction of a cumulative frequency table is useful in determining the:
(a) Mean
(b) Median
(c) Mode
(d) All of these
Answer: (b) Median
Median uses cumulative frequencies.
8. Which of the following cannot be determined graphically?
(a) Mean
(b) Median
(c) Mode
(d) None of these
Answer: (a) Mean
Median via Ogive, Mode via Histogram. Mean has no direct graph.
9. The abscissa of the point of intersection of the less than type and of the more than type cumulative frequency curves of a grouped data gives its:
(a) Mean
(b) Median
(c) Mode
(d) Range
Answer: (b) Median
Intersection of Ogives gives Median.
10. If the mean of first n natural numbers is 5n/9, then n = ?
(a) 9
(b) 10
(c) 11
(d) 12
Answer: (a) 9
Sum = n(n+1)/2. Mean = (n+1)/2.
(n+1)/2 = 5n/9 => 9n+9 = 10n => n=9.
