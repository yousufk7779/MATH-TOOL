Chapter 6: Triangles
Multiple Choice Questions
1. Two figures having the same shape but not necessarily the same size are called:
(A) Similar figures (B) Congruent figures (C) Equal figures (D) Parallel figures
Correct Answer: (A)
2. All circles are __________ to each other.
(A) Congruent (B) Similar (C) Equal (D) None of these
Correct Answer: (B)
3. Two polygons of the same number of sides are similar if their corresponding angles are:
(A) Equal (B) Proportional (C) Supplementary (D) Complementary
Correct Answer: (A)
4. All equilateral triangles are:
(A) Congruent (B) Similar (C) Isosceles (D) Right-angled
Correct Answer: (B)
5. If a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle to intersect the other two sides, the other two sides are divided in the same ratio. This is known as:
(A) Pythagoras Theorem (B) Basic Proportionality Theorem (C) Mid-point Theorem (D) Area Theorem
Correct Answer: (B)
6. If in two triangles, corresponding angles are equal, then their corresponding sides are in the same ratio and hence the two triangles are similar. This criterion is:
(A) SSS similarity (B) SAS similarity (C) AAA similarity (D) Rhs similarity
Correct Answer: (C)
7. If one angle of a triangle is equal to one angle of another triangle and the sides including these angles are proportional, the triangles are similar by:
(A) SSS similarity (B) SAS similarity (C) AAA similarity (D) AAS similarity
Correct Answer: (B)
8. In triangle ABC, if DE || BC, then AD/DB is equal to:
(A) AE/EC (B) AC/AE (C) AB/AD (D) BC/DE
Correct Answer: (A)
9. Which of the following is NOT a criterion for similarity of triangles?
(A) AAA (B) SAS (C) SSS (D) SSA
Correct Answer: (D)
10. If the corresponding sides of two similar triangles are in the ratio 2:3, then their areas are in the ratio:
(A) 2:3 (B) 4:9 (C) 8:27 (D) \(\sqrt{2}\) : \(\sqrt{3}\)
Correct Answer: (B)
