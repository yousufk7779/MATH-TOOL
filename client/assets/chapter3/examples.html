Example 1. Check graphically whether x + 3y = 6 and 2x - 3y = 12 is consistent. If so, solve them graphically.
Graph of x+3y=6 & 2x-3y=12
Solution:
Given equations:
x + 3y = 6 ...(1)
2x - 3y = 12 ...(2)
Observation for Graph:
For equation (1):
x = 6 - 3y
x 6 0
y 0 2
For equation (2):
2x = 12 + 3y
x = 12 + 3y2
x 6 0
y 0 -4
Plotting the points, both lines intersect at the point (6, 0).
Since there is a unique point of intersection, the pair of equations is consistent.
Solution: x = 6, y = 0
Example 2. Half the perimeter of a rectangular garden is 36 m. If the length is 4 m more than its width, find the dimensions.
Given:
Half Perimeter = 36 m
Length (L) = Width (W) + 4
To Find:
Length and Width of the garden.
Solution:
Let length = L and width = W.
L = W + 4 ...(1)
Half Perimeter = L + W = 36 ...(2)
Substitute (1) in (2):
(W + 4) + W = 36
2W + 4 = 36
2W = 32
W = 16 m
Substitute W = 16 in (1):
L = 16 + 4 = 20 m
Length = 20 m, Width = 16 m
Example 3. Solve the following pair of equations by substitution method:
7x - 15y = 2
x + 2y = 3
Solution:
7x - 15y = 2 ...(1)
x + 2y = 3 ...(2)
From (2):
x = 3 - 2y ...(3)
Substitute x in (1):
7(3 - 2y) - 15y = 2
21 - 14y - 15y = 2
21 - 29y = 2
-29y = -19
y = 1929
Substitute y in (3):
x = 3 - 2(1929)
x = 3 - 3829
x = 87 - 3829
x = 4929
x = 4929, y = 1929
