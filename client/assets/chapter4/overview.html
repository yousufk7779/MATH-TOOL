Chapter 4: Quadratic Equations
1. Introduction
A quadratic equation in the variable x is an equation of the form ax² + bx + c = 0, where a, b, c are real numbers and a ≠ 0.
2. Standard Form
The standard form is:
ax² + bx + c = 0, a ≠ 0
Here:
a is the coefficient of x²
b is the coefficient of x
c is the constant term
3. Methods to Solve
1. Factorisation: Splitting the middle term to form linear factors and equating each to zero.
2. Completing the Square: Converting the equation into a perfect square form (x + a)² - b² = 0.
3. Quadratic Formula: Using the direct formula to find roots.
x = -b ± √D2a
Where D = b² - 4ac is called the Discriminant.
4. Nature of Roots
The nature of roots depends on the value of the Discriminant D = b² - 4ac:
Case 1: D > 0
Two distinct real roots exist.
Roots are -b + √D2a and -b - √D2a.
Case 2: D = 0
Two equal real roots exist (Real and Coincident).
Roots are -b2a, -b2a.
Case 3: D < 0
No real roots exist (Roots are imaginary).
