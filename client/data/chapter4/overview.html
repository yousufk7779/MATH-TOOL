Coordinate geometry is a branch of mathematics that defines the position of a point in a plane using two numbers, called coordinates. This system was developed by the French mathematician René Descartes, which is why it is also known as the Cartesian system.

The Cartesian System

In a plane, we take two perpendicular lines: a horizontal line called the X-axis and a vertical line called the Y-axis. The point where these two lines intersect is called the Origin, denoted by O (0, 0).

Coordinates of a Point

Any point P in the plane is represented by an ordered pair (x, y), where:

  • • Abscissa: The x-coordinate represents the distance of the point from the Y-axis.
  • • Ordinate: The y-coordinate represents the distance of the point from the X-axis.

The Four Quadrants

The axes divide the plane into four parts, called quadrants:

Quadrant Sign of (x, y) Description
Quadrant I (+, +) Upper Right
Quadrant II (-, +) Upper Left
Quadrant III (-, -) Lower Left
Quadrant IV (+, -) Lower Right

Points on the X-axis have a y-coordinate of 0, i.e., (x, 0). Similarly, points on the Y-axis have an x-coordinate of 0, i.e., (0, y).
